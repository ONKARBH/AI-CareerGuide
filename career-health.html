Physician Assistant

$115,000 median salary 31% projected growth Master's degree

Physician assistants (PAs) practice medicine on teams with physicians, surgeons, and other healthcare workers. They examine, diagnose, and treat patients under the supervision of a physician.

Key Responsibilities

  • Take medical histories and examine patients
  • Order and interpret diagnostic tests
  • Diagnose injuries and illnesses
  • Provide treatment and prescribe medication
  • Educate patients on health maintenance

Skills Required

Medical Knowledge Communication Empathy Problem Solving Attention to Detail Teamwork

Career Path

PAs typically need a master's degree from an accredited educational program. All states require PAs to be licensed. Many PAs gain healthcare experience before applying to PA programs, often as nurses, EMTs, or paramedics.

Quick Facts
Education: Master's Degree
Work Experience: 2+ years healthcare
Job Outlook: 31% (Much faster than average)
